The artisan ardorned with countless gifts

The Italian polymath, often credited as the founder of the High Reinassance and believed by some to be the smartest man to have ever lived, was actively talented as:

  1. A painter
  2. A draughtsman
  3. An engineer
  4. A scientist
  5. A theorist
  6. A sculptor
  7. An architect

He made drawings and notes on a variety of subjects, including anatomy, astronomy, botany, cartography, painting, and palaeontology. His paintings and inventions made him the leading man of the Italian Renaissance period.

The master of innumerable expertise:

  • Gave us art (his most famous works include the Mona Lisa and The Last Supper)
  • Helped us understand human anatomy
  • Foresaw the age of flight 400 years before it became reality
He was creating sketches of machines which look like bicycles and helicopters way before the Wright Brothers took flight on December 17, 1903.

His achievements cross so many disciplines that it is difficult to showcase his talents in just a few sentences.
